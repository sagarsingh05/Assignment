
## **1. What is SEO? Explain the Importance of SEO.**  
**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)** is the practice of optimizing web pages to **rank higher** in search engine results (Google, Bing, etc.), increasing visibility and traffic.

### **Importance of SEO:**
1. **Higher Website Traffic** – Increases the number of visitors through **organic search results**.  
2. **Better User Experience** – SEO improves site speed, mobile-friendliness, and navigation.  
3. **Credibility & Trust** – Websites that rank higher are seen as more **trustworthy**.  
4. **Cost-Effective Marketing** – Unlike paid ads, **organic traffic is free** and sustainable.  
5. **Competitive Advantage** – Helps businesses outrank competitors in search engines.  

---


### **Explanation of SEO Tags:**
- `<title>` – Descriptive and includes main **keywords**.  
- `<meta name="description">` – Short, compelling summary for **Google search results**.  
- `<meta name="keywords">` – Relevant **keywords** (though Google relies less on this).  
- `<meta name="author">` – Specifies the **content creator**.  

---

## **3. Benefits of Using `<meta>` Tags**
1. **Improves SEO Ranking** – The **title & description** meta tags help search engines understand the page content.  
2. **Enhances Click-Through Rate (CTR)** – Well-written **meta descriptions** encourage users to click.  
3. **Better Social Media Sharing** – Meta tags improve **how links appear** when shared on social media.  
4. **Device Responsiveness** – `<meta name="viewport">` ensures mobile-friendly design.  
5. **Faster Indexing** – Helps **search engines index** pages correctly.

---

### **Why This Helps SEO?**
- **`<header>`** – Defines the main **heading** for clarity.  
- **`<nav>`** – Organizes navigation links for **better indexing**.  
- **`<section>` & `<article>`** – Improves **content readability** and ranking.  
- **`<footer>`** – Contains important information.

## **5. What is a Favicon?**
A **Favicon (Favorite Icon)** is a small **icon displayed in the browser tab** next to the page title. It helps users identify a website **visually**.

### **Example of Adding a Favicon in HTML**
```html
<head>
    <link rel="icon" type="image/png" href="favicon.png">
    <title>Website with Favicon</title>
</head>
```
### **Explanation:**
- `rel="icon"` – Specifies it's a favicon.  
- `type="image/png"` – Specifies the file format.  
- `href="favicon.png"` – Link to the **favicon image**.
